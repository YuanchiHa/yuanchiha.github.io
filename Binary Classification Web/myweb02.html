ML Classification in Biology

About Framework


Given a coarse-grained biology rule with scarce data, is there a way to predict the result without doing the nontrivial measurements?

    Framework Requirement
  • The system has 2 qualitative behaviors B1 & B2.
  • The behaviors are determined by 2 competing forces F1 & F2
  • F1 is easy to quantify; F2 is hard to quantify.
  • F1/F2 is correlated with some quantitative behavior B3.

    Measurement Requirement
  • There exists a space V that many instances of B1 & B2 are observed.
  • V of every B1 & B2 is measured
  • F1 is measured for every instance.

    Returned Results
  • Quantified F2.
  • Prediction of B3

Examples


1. Mutualism in Microbial Community

    Collapse or Coexist Framework
  • The system's 2 qualitative behaviors: Collapse & Coexist.
  • The 2 determining forces: Effective Benefit & Stress
  • Stress is easy to quantify; Effective Benefit not so much.
  • Effective Benefit/Stress is correlated with quantity: final cell density.

    Collapse or Coexist Measurement
  • The space V: stress modulator IPTG & benefit modulator aTC
  • V of every behavior controlled and measured
  • Stress measured for every instance.

    Returned Results
  • Quantified Effective Benefit.
  • Prediction of Final Density

Reference: A unifying framework for interpreting and predicting mutualistic systems
